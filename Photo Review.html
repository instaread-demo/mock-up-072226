Bowness Photography Prize finalists announced

Listen to audio version of this article
Sponsored by
0:00 / 0:56
Instaread

The Museum of Australian Photography has announced the 50 selected finalists for the 2026 William & Winifred Bowness Photography Prize, which is now worth $50,000 for the winner.


Julie RRAP: ‘Un-becoming monument #4 2026’ from the series ‘Un-becoming monuments’. Digital print courtesy of the artist. One of the finalists in this year’s competition.

Judges Natasha Bullock, Petrina Hicks and Anouska Phizacklea considered over 750 entries before choosing the finalists, who will compete for cash prizes of $50,000 for the winner and $2,500 cash awarded to two honourable mentions. Shortlisted artists will be paid loan fees for the exhibition of their works. The selected artists are as follows: Robert Ashton, Aurélie Beeston, Madeline Bishop, Danica Chappell, Maree Clarke, Timothy Coad, Nyree Cox, Ellen Dahl, J Davies, Penelope Davis, Tamara Dean, Yask Desai, damian dillon, Nathanael Edwards, Odette England, Adam Ferguson, Murray Fredericks, Silvi Glattauer, John Gollings, Dr Kristian Häggblom, Paul Harris, Leo Razak Harunah, Siri Hayes, Crystal Hua, The Huxleys, Ingvar Kenne, Shea Kirk, Bec Lorrimer, Clare McCracken, Heather Hesterman & Andrew Ferris, Kent Morris, Rebecca Najdowski, Harry Nankin, Brett Nannup, Ruth O’Leary, Felix Oliver, Jake Preval, Kiah Pullens, Clare Rae, Julie Rrap, Damien Shen & James Tylor, Scotty So, Valerie Sparks, Tobias Titz, Teo Treloar, Shan Turner-Carroll, Julia Rose van Haren, Justine Varga, Drew Waters, Lydia Wegner and Grace Wood.

From this group, an artist will also be selected for the $10,000 Wai Tang Commissioning Award with the opportunity to exhibit a body of work throughout next year’s Bowness Photography Prize season. This award is proudly supported by Kee Wong and Stacey Wang. The RACV People’s Choice winner will be awarded a $5,000 cash prize: voting will commence from the exhibition’s opening day on 12 September 2026. The exhibition of the finalist and winning images will run from 12 September to 8 November at the Museum of Australian Photography, 860 Ferntree Gully Rd, Wheelers Hill, Victoria.
