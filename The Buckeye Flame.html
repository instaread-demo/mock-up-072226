AudioThis News is So Gay (7/22/26): Amplifying “Pride on Full Display at the Obama Presidential Center”

Featuring: Anna DeShawn (E3 Radio / Queer News / The Qube)
By Ken Schneck ● Podcasts, Queer Joy ● July 22, 2026
Listen to audio version of this article

In this week’s episode of This News is So Gay, we’re amplifying a special edition of the Queer News podcast, “Pride on Full Display at the Obama Presidential Center,” featuring the incomparable Anna DeShawn.

Anna is a media visionary who creates unique spaces and places for black and indigenous people of color and for queer and trans people of color. In 2009, Anna founded E3 Radio, an online radio station that plays queer music and reports on queer news with a focus on intersectionality. In 2020, Anna co-founded The Qube, a podcast production company and curated platform to discover the best music and podcasts by BIPOC, queer, and trans creatives. In March 2023, Anna’s podcast Queer News won an Ambie Award, which basically like the Oscars of podcasting.

Support for This News is So Gay comes from the LGBTQ+ Opportunity Fund of the Cleveland Foundation, leveraging philanthropy to empower LGBTQ+ communities.

Author

Ken Schneck

Dr. Ken Schneck (he/him) is the editor of The Buckeye Flame, Ohio’s LGBTQ+ newsroom. For this work, he was honored with the Sarah Pettit Memorial Award for National LGBTQ+ Journalist of the Year. He is the author of “Seriously, What Am I Doing Here? The Adventures of a Wondering and Wandering Gay Jew” and three LGBTQ+ Ohio history books. Schneck spent 25 years working in higher education, including 10 years as a dean of students and 13 years as a tenured professor of education. He is a graduate of the Executive Program in News Innovation and Leadership at the Craig Newmark Graduate School of Journalism at the City University of New York.
