Woodbury Offers Financial Help To Remove Emerald Ash Borer-Damaged Trees

by Beth-Ann Bloom
Published July 21, 2026
Listen to audio version of this article
Sponsored by
0:00 / 1:23
Instaread

A new income-based grant program will help Woodbury residents pay for the removal of dead or damaged ash trees on their property. Applications should be submitted as soon as possible because funding is limited.

Removing dead and diseased ash trees is important for public safety and protecting other trees. Ash trees killed by emerald ash borer dry out rapidly and become extremely brittle. They can lose branches or collapse unexpectedly, threatening homes, power lines and pedestrians. Dead and dying ash trees also serve as breeding grounds for larvae, allowing the beetles to spread to nearby healthy trees.

Who Qualifies

Qualifications for assistance with trees damaged by emerald ash borer include:

  • A tree located on owner-occupied property.
  • Household income within U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development limits based on household size.
  • A copy of the most recent property tax statement.
  • IRS Form 1040 tax returns for all adults in the household.
  • Verification of student status for any adult students.

Completed applications will be reviewed by city staff following federal grant procedures, and homeowners will be notified within three to four weeks. Those who qualify will receive instructions on obtaining bids from a tree service within two weeks, documenting the removal and receiving reimbursement.

Residents who think they may qualify for the program can contact Deb Harrison, Woodbury parks and forestry technician, with questions.
