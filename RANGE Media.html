Where do the council candidates stand on Medicare, the surveillance state, supporting rural constituents and more? Here’s a bite-sized snapshot before you get your ballots!

By Erin Sellers
Published: July 21, 2026, 5:34 pm
Share to X Share to Bluesky Share to Facebook Share to LinkedIn Share by email

Washington’s Fifth Congressional District stretches across the eastern edge of the state, spanning more than 15,000 square miles and encompassing 10 of the most rural counties in the state. Twelve candidates, including incumbent Congressman Michael Baumgartner, are currently running to represent the roughly 800,000 who call the district home. 

We asked all twelve candidates to give us brief, clear answers to 20 questions about some of the key issues for voters this year, like: should individual ICE agents be held accountable for their actions? Should the US continue funding the Israeli military? What regulations should be placed on AI usage and data center citing at the federal level? 

All that and more in the handy chart below, and if this isn’t enough to help you make your choice, don’t worry — we’ll be posting stories on the race, linking to candidate forums and sharing coverage by other outlets that you can use to become the most informed voter in Spokane.

Nine of the twelve candidates responded to our questionnaire, and their answers are included verbatim below. Note: for some questions, candidates were asked to choose solely yes, no or other.
