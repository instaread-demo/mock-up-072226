    Poetry: Tunes on Tuesday, What a Wonderful World

    Newberg local Janet Cain submitted a poem written after experiencing Tunes on Tuesday on July 14, 2026.

    Uninhibited children 
    dance circles around
    inhibited parents 
    Twirling twirly skirts 
    curls, braids, pony tails 
    bouncing to the beat
    cartwheels of delight 

    Boys run 
    in circles
    in chase
    airplane arms zoom
    tag, you’re it
    flying on rhythm’s tail

    Old guy in Rolling Stones Tee
    guides her in sensible flats
    rehearsed in-sync moves
    dance partners for life

    Music equalizes an equitable blessing
    volume increase
    heads bob in unity
    Sam Cook 
    guitar rift
    Mariachi 
    feet tapping 
    sax solo
    slapping thighs in time 
    drum throbs
    babes in arms smile
    bass sets the beat
    dogs grin 
    organ runs up the keyboard
    Don’t know much about history
    dancers appear
    Don’t know much biology
    children spin on grassy dance floor
    But I do know that I love you
    shoulders shaking
    And I know that if you love me too 
    everyone now 
    What a wonderful world this would be

    —Janet Cain 2026
